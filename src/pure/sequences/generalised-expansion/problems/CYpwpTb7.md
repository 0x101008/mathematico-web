Let
$$
f(x) = \frac{1}{x^2 - 5x + 6}
$$

 1. Find the partial fraction decomposition of $f(x)$

 1. Hence, find the binomial expansion of $f(x)$ up to and including the term in $x^3$

---

$$
f(x) = \frac{1}{x-3} - \frac{1}{x-2}
$$
You can expand
$$
(x-3)^{-1}
$$
and
$$
(x-2)^-1
$$
separately.
