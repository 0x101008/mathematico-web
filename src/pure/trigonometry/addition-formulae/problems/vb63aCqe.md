 1. Show that, for acute $\theta,$
    $$
    \cos \theta = \sqrt{1 - \sin^2 \theta}
    $$
    and hence show that
    $$
    \cos( \arcsin y ) = \sqrt{1 - y^2}
    $$

 1. Express
    $$
    \arcsin \alpha + \arcsin \beta
    $$
    as a single $\arcsin.$

 1. Express
    $$
    \arccos \alpha + \arccos \beta
    $$
    as a single $\arccos.$
