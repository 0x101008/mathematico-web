 Define
 $$
 \delta = g(x+h) - g(x)
 $$

 1. Show that
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    \,\, & \frac{f(g(x+h)) - f(g(x))}{h} \\[1em]
    = \,\, & \frac{f\left(g(x) + \delta\right) - f(g(x))}{\delta} \, \frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h}
    \end{aligned}
    $$

 1. Explain why
    $$
    \delta \rightarrow 0 \, \text{ as } \, h \rightarrow 0
    $$

 1. Hence, use first principles to prove the chain rule:
    $$
    \frac{d}{dx} f(g(x)) = f'(g(x))g'(x)
    $$
