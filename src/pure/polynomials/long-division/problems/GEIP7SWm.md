Given that $(x - 3)$ is a factor of the cubic
$$
x^3 + x - 30
$$
show that $x = 3$ is the only real solution to the equation
$$
x^3 + x - 30 = 0
$$

---

Use long division to get it in the form
$$
(x-3)q(x) = 0
$$
where $q$ is a quadratic.

---

Use the discriminant on $q.$
