Using the substitution $x = \sin\theta,$ find
$$
\int \frac{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}{x^2} \, dx
$$

---

You should get
$$
\frac{\cos^2(x)}{\sin^2(x)}
$$
Write this as
$$
\frac{1-\sin^2(x)}{\cos^2(x)}
$$
and split it up into two separate fractions. You can use standard integrals from here!
