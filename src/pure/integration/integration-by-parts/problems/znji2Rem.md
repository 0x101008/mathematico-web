---

You should be able to use integration by parts to show that
$$
\int x \sin(x) \, dx = \sin(x) - x \cos(x)
$$
That means
$$
\int_{a}^{b} x \sin(x) \, dx = \left[ \sin(x) - x \cos(x) \right]_{a}^{b}
$$
